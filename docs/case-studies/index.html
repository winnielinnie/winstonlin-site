OCI DevOps / CI-CD

Service Installer

Shortened regional software delivery from roughly 3 months to 30 days by turning deployment sequencing into a product instead of tribal knowledge.

Problem

OCI service launches depended on a tangle of manual sequencing, service-specific runbooks, and hidden dependencies. Even when teams knew the path, the process was slow and brittle.

What I did

  • Helped frame the problem as a reusable orchestration product rather than one more internal process fix.
  • Worked through where sequencing could be standardized versus where teams needed controlled escape hatches.
  • Pushed toward a model that made dependencies visible enough to reason about instead of leaving them buried in institutional memory.

Why it was hard

  • The hard part was not automation by itself. It was deciding what deserved to become platform behavior versus local exception handling.
  • Internal platforms always face the temptation to encode every special case. That usually makes them worse.

Outcome

  • Regional delivery timelines dropped from about 3 months to roughly 30 days.
  • The work created a clearer foundation for internal teams to plan launches without having to rediscover the same dependency chains.

OCI DevOps / CI-CD

Feature Registry with Health and Dependency Tracing

Built a clearer operating picture for features, ownership, and dependency health, helping cut root-cause analysis time by 60%.

Problem

When things broke, people could often identify the symptom quickly but not the ownership chain, dependency path, or blast radius. That made investigation slower than it needed to be.

What I did

  • Helped shape the registry around real operational questions instead of turning it into a passive inventory system.
  • Focused the design on health, dependency tracing, and ownership visibility rather than accumulating metadata for its own sake.
  • Spent a lot of time on what someone under pressure would need to know first.

Why it was hard

  • Registries are easy to overbuild and easy to let drift into irrelevance.
  • The challenge was making the information close enough to reality that engineers would still trust it when they actually needed it.

Outcome

  • Root-cause analysis time dropped by about 60%.
  • The work made dependency questions far more answerable during incidents and rollout debugging.

OCI Functions

AI-Assisted OCI Functions Workflows

Worked on AI-assisted deployment and troubleshooting flows for OCI Functions to reduce setup drag and make platform recovery paths clearer.

Problem

A lot of friction in serverless adoption comes from the first-run experience: packaging, environment setup, troubleshooting, and the gap between a docs path and a working deployment.

What I did

  • Explored AI-assisted deployment and troubleshooting flows using MCP-style integrations and reusable skills.
  • Treated the problem as a workflow-design problem, not just a model-response problem.
  • Focused on making the output operationally useful: what should happen next, what failed, and how to recover.

Why it was hard

  • AI features are easy to demo and much harder to trust in real developer workflows.
  • The bar was not 'did the assistant say something plausible.' The bar was whether it reduced friction without creating new ambiguity.

Outcome

  • Created a credible path for AI-assisted setup and triage instead of treating AI as a sidecar novelty.
  • Sharpened my view that trustworthy AI products need strong recovery paths, explicit boundaries, and good workflow design.
