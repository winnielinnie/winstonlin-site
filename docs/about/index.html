About

A little about me.

A simple page for the broader context behind the work.

I’m based in San Francisco and work in product, mostly around cloud and developer tools. Day to day, I spend a lot of time thinking about how to make things simpler and easier to use, especially for people building and shipping products.

Outside of that, I invest in and occasionally advise early-stage startups in both the U.S. and Asia. I like being around people building things from scratch and thinking through how something goes from an idea to something that actually works.

Background

Work, place, and a bit of context.

I spend most of my time in product, especially around cloud, infrastructure, and developer tools. I like work that sits close to real use, where the challenge is not just building something capable, but making it understandable and usable for the people shipping with it.

Outside of my day job, I invest in and occasionally advise early-stage startups. A lot of that is simply an interest in how things get built, how good judgment compounds over time, and how ideas turn into something durable.

Outside of work

A few things I spend time on outside the day job.

I grew up between Taiwan and Orange County, which gave me a mix of perspectives early on and made me comfortable moving between different environments. I later studied at UC Berkeley Haas, where I got more interested in the overlap between business and technology.

Outside of work, I try to stay active and get outdoors when I can, whether that means hiking around the Bay Area, exploring new places, or just getting out of the city for a bit. I also shoot photography on Fujifilm, mostly as a way to slow down and capture things that feel real in the moment.

I grew up playing sports and still play basketball and volleyball when I can. Lately I’ve also been spending more time thinking about investing, real estate, and how small decisions add up over time.
