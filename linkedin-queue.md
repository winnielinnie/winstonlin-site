# LinkedIn Queue

## Draft 1

One thing I keep coming back to in developer platforms:

Most adoption problems are not caused by one giant missing feature.

They come from a pile of smaller friction points:
- setup that takes too long
- docs that miss the moment of confusion
- feedback loops that are slower than the alternative
- failure states that are technically accurate but not very helpful

Teams usually notice the big capability gaps first because they are easier to name.
But a lot of trust is won or lost in the first hour.

## Draft 2

Migration readiness is underrated as a product quality signal.

If a platform is hard to migrate to, that usually means some combination of the onboarding, packaging, docs, defaults, or troubleshooting model still needs work.

That matters beyond migration too.
The same improvements that help a workload land also tend to make the product better for everyone already there.

## Draft 3

I still like small Python tools.

Not everything needs to become a platform.

Sometimes the right answer is a 150-line script that removes one recurring annoyance from the week and is simple enough that someone else can pick it up in ten minutes.
